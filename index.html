About

My research bridges architecture, building performance, and AI. I study how buildings can act as human-aware, grid-interactive infrastructures under climate extremes: quantifying heat exposure and health risk, embedding these boundaries into semantic ontologies, and using agentic LLM frameworks to control façades, HVAC, and PV systems safely and flexibly.

  • M.Eng. in Architecture, Harbin Institute of Technology (recommended admission), focusing on vision-perception-based adaptive façades and occupant-centric glare control.
  • Remote research assistant with HKUST, developing tool-augmented and multi-agent LLM systems (Planner–Coder–Verifier) for building time-series data management and automation.
  • Current PhD research direction: health-aware semantic–agentic digital twins integrating heat–health modeling, Brick/IFC ontologies, and DRL-based flexibility control for Grid-Interactive Efficient Buildings (GEBs).

Selected publications

  1. An adaptive façade control strategy based on real-time evaluation of light comfort integrating gaze estimation and expression recognition. Building Simulation, Accepted (JCR Q1)

    Proposes a vision-perception-based adaptive façade combining gaze tracking and facial expression recognition to evaluate visual comfort and drive zonal façade actuation.

  2. PV-GPT: query the PV data using natural language. Journal of Building Engineering, Accepted (JCR Q1)

    Designs a natural-language interface and LLM pipeline for querying SolarEdge PV monitoring data, enabling complex temporal and diagnostic questions for non-expert users.

  3. A Multi-objective Optimization Framework for Designing Residential Green Space between Buildings. Sustainable Cities and Society, Accepted (JCR Q1)

    Integrates outdoor thermal comfort, daylighting, and greenery metrics into a unified optimization workflow.

  4. Application of human activity recognition in smart homes for elderly care: a literature review. CEB-ASC 2024, Oral

    Reviews HAR pipelines and deployment challenges for real-world elderly-care environments.

  5. PV-GPT2.0: a multi-agent chatbot to query PV data. Manuscript in preparation

    Extends PV-GPT with a Planner–Coder–Verifier multi-agent workflow and benchmark tasks.
